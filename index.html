About US

Use of energy-efficient infrastructure and appliances in buildings to reduce energy consumption is the traditional and dominant approach taken to cut down carbon emissions. An alternative approach that promises significant change is the promotion of energy consumption awareness and behavioural change on people living or working inside buildings. Though sounding too simplistic, this approach gives us a better practical solution, helping us to avoid integration of new appliances in the existing infrastructures, which leads to high implementation costs. Often, young people are very sensitive to environmental protection. Consequently, raising awareness among students is much easier than with any other group of citizens. For this reason, we plan on creating an interactive IoT based platform, explicitly tailored for school environments to achieve greater energy efficiency among faculty, staff and students. The proposed solution consists of an ecosystem of sensors, which collects real-time data on energy consumption, motion/activity and environmental parameters such as temperature, noise level, relative humidity, CO2 concentration, luminosity to provide services on monitoring and profiling of the energy consumption in different sections of the targeted building. Further works can focus on developing an easy-to-use and interactive gamified (reward-based) software framework model which can analyse data over some time and use this analysis to educate students and improve the usage.
